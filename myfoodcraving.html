The problem

Most nutrition software stops at advice.

A meal plan can be nutritionally sound and still fail because the user does not know what to cook, does not have the ingredients, does not have time, or simply does not want another static list.

MyFoodCraving treats recommendation and execution as one continuous product problem. It explains why a meal fits, turns that meal into guided cooking, and can continue into a local chef experience.

Product surface

From nutrition needs to food on the table.

Diet identity, allergies and intolerances are hard eligibility boundaries. Other goals are balanced transparently.

01

Personalized daily meals

Build breakfast, lunch, snack and dinner options around a person's current nutrition profile and activity needs.

02

Health-marker prioritization

Optional measured markers can change which nutritional goals receive greater priority without becoming medical advice.

03

Explainable reasons

Recommendations state why a dish fits: nutrients, constraints and the part of the day's open need it helps address.

04

Diversity by design

Re-rank options to avoid near-duplicates and rotate recently eaten meals away from the front of the list.

05

Guided cooking

Turn a selected recommendation directly into step-by-step cooking instead of sending the user to another app.

06

Chef marketplace

Discover recipe-relevant local chefs, review their signals, and move from meal choice to a constrained booking flow.

The meal loop

Understand. Recommend. Explain. Cook.

01

Set the boundaries

Diet identity, allergies, intolerances and explicit dislikes define what is eligible.

02

Prioritize the day's needs

Energy, protein, safety caps, optional health markers and micronutrient gaps shape the score.

03

Explain the fit

The recommendation communicates the concrete nutrition reasons behind the ranking.

04

Finish the action

Cook through the recipe yourself or move into the local chef flow.
