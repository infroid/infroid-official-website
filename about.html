The studio

Different domains. The same product standard.

Our work has taken us into AI development infrastructure, governed analytics, personal health and nutrition. Those industries look unrelated until you examine the failure mode underneath them.

Agents act on stale context. Analysts can give answers without inspectable evidence. Health information fragments across care moments. Nutrition advice stops before the meal is made.

We keep returning to the gap between information and useful action.

“Understand the context. Make the reasoning visible. Help the next action happen.”

That is the thread connecting ContextHub, EDP Assist, EaseMyDisease and MyFoodCraving.

What we do not bend on

Four product principles.

01 / CLARITY

Understand before automating.

A fast answer to the wrong question is still wrong. Define the context, uncertainty and boundaries first.

02 / GUARDRAILS

Constraints are product features.

Read-only access, explicit approvals, user-controlled sharing and hard eligibility rules belong in the product architecture.

03 / CONTINUITY

Finish the loop.

Context should reach the agent. Analysis should reach the decision. Health information should travel with the person. A meal recommendation should be possible to cook.

04 / TASTE

Useful should feel inevitable.

The interface, language and interaction model should remove explanation rather than demand more of it.

Why the portfolio matters

Independence first. Composition second.

We do not force four products into a suite. Each must be a complete answer on its own.

The portfolio becomes stronger when context can move safely.

A health product should not expose an entire vault to a food product. An analytics tool should not become a hidden backend inside every app. A context system should not absorb domain data it does not own.

The better model is a scoped handoff: a small, typed package of context or evidence with purpose, provenance, consent and expiry.
